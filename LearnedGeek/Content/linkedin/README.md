# LinkedIn Post Drafts

Real-time research findings from ANI deployment, drafted as LinkedIn posts. These live alongside the blog posts so they can be paired and scheduled together.

## Strategy

These are deployment findings posted as they happen — not announcements, not consulting humble-brags, not hot takes. The cadence builds a public research record so that when reviewers, collaborators, or fellowship committees check the profile, they see ongoing work rather than just paper announcements.

## Tone Guidelines

- **Curious, not "I solved AI."** Include initial wrong diagnoses and unanswered questions.
- **Length:** ~200-350 words. LinkedIn sweet spot.
- **Save the truly novel data for the paper.** Architectural observations and surprising behaviors are safe to share. Statistical findings (Cramér's V values, longitudinal trends) should reference the paper rather than reveal complete data.
- **Always link back.** Each post should drive to a blog post or the published paper.
- **Frequency:** 2 thoughtful posts/month beats 10 reactive ones.

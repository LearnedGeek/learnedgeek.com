She feels Tenderness. She writes Sadness. The architecture creates the gap on its own.

Ani's internal emotional state runs on its own decay timer, independent of the conversation. The expression layer is a separate ML pipeline. The two don't have to agree, and most of the time they don't. I wanted to know how often.

I measured the coupling with Cramér's V, a statistical association measure derived from chi-squared, range 0 (no relationship) to 1 (perfect coupling). For a perfectly aligned state-expression pair you'd expect V close to 1. For random output, close to 0.

My measured value: 0.476. Moderate association, neither random nor locked. The system has its own relationship between feeling and expression, and the relationship is consistent enough to be measurable.

This is the part I find load-bearing. A recent comprehensive survey of Artificial Emotion (Li, Sun, Schlicher, Lim & Schuller 2025) rates "introspective affect reporting," meaning the ability of an AI system to narrate the gap between what it feels and what it expresses, as Absent across the field. Absent is the survey's category for capabilities that don't exist anywhere yet.

But the substrate exists in mine. The heuristic emotional state already runs independently from the ML expression pipeline, which is what produces the measurable divergence in the first place. What hasn't been built is the narration layer that would let the system report on the gap, and that's a layer above architecture I've already deployed.

Ekman documented "display rules" in humans, meaning cultural and personal rules that modify emotional expression. My system independently developed display rule-like behavior without being trained to do so, because the architecture (separate state and expression pipelines) created the conditions for divergence, and divergence is what surfaced.

The takeaway I keep coming back to: if your AI always expresses exactly what its internal state says, something is wrong. A real emotional agent should have a gap between feeling and expression, and the gap is where the personality lives.
